A slideshow of images from the 2012 ISE PI Meeting held March 14-16th at the Omni Shoreham Hotel in Washington, DC.

Dennis Schatz, NSF ISE Program Officer and Cluster Coordinator, welcomes attendees and introduces fellow program officers and Dr. Joan Ferrini-Mundy.

Dr. Joan Ferrini-Mundy, Assistant Director, Directorate for Education and Human Resources, welcomes meeting attendees and provides updates on the ISE Program.

CAISE Cognizant Program Officer, Al DeSena provides opening remarks about the Informal Science Education Program and CAISE.

Nancy Maryboy and David Begay, PI and Co-PI of the Cosmic Serpent project, look over the meeting program with Ben Dickow, CAISE "Entree" initiative leader.

Barinetta Scott, PI of the Human Condition project, participates in discussions about STEM Media.

Pamela Hawkins, Branch Chief, Division of Grants and Agreements answers questions about award management during technical assistance session.

Laura Buckley (center right) and Angela Turner (far right), both of the NSF DGA, answer questions on award management.

Sandra Toro Martell, NSF ISE Program Officer, shares the NSF perspective on the Online Project Monitoring System (OPMS) during technical assistance sessions.

Gary Silverstein, Senior Study Director at Westat, presents on the progress of the Online Project Monitoring System (OPMS).

Monica Smith, Exhibition Program Manager for the Lemelson Center at the National Museum of American History (right) discusses the OPMS with Hannah Putman of Westat.

Taking a break between technical assistance sessions on Wednesday, March 14th.

Marilyn Johnson from OMSI, Larry Bell of Museum of Science Boston and Steve Curwood of the World Media Foundation.

Trevor Nesbit, CAISE Web Strategist searches the Informal Commons and ISE Timeline with Al DeSena.

Barinetta Scott (second from left) talks with John Falk, CAISE co-PI (far right) during the welcoming reception.

Enjoying the spring weather during the Wednesday evening welcoming reception on.

Julieanna Richardson, PI for ScienceMakers, poses with Sandra Welch, NSF ISE Program Officer, during the welcoming reception.

Kirsten Ellenbogen (center), Co-Principal Investigator for CAISE, Joe Heimlich of Ohio State University (left) and Chris Myers, PI of the Wild Research and Saving Species projects during the welcoming reception.

Jamie Bell, CAISE PI and Project Director, opens the meeting on Thursday morning.

NSF ISE Program Officers pose before visiting project posters on Thursday morning.

Lee Gutkind of Arizona State University speaks with grant writer Carol Inman about the To Think To Write To publish conference project.

Julieanna Richardson (right) shares information about her project, ScienceMakers.

Marlene Klineman of TERC (center) discusses her Math off the Shelf project with poster session attendees.

Meeting attendees participate in surveys for evaluation of the ISE Program with SRI International.

Dennis Schatz moderates a panel discussion with Maureen Callanan from UC Santa Cruz, Daniel Rockmore from Dartmouth, Chris Myers from Miami University of Ohio and Michael Heithaus from Florida International University.

Maureen Callanan answers a question from Dennis Schatz during a lunchtime panel on scientists and public engagement.

Lynn Dierking of Oregon State University asks a question during the lunchtime panel on scientists and public engagement.

Darrell Porcello of Lawrence Hall of Science (LHS) presents information about the Informal Commons, a web resource developed by CAISE, Ideum, and LHS.

Sherry Hsi from LHS presents the ISE Timeline, an interactive and dynamic tool that documents the history of informal science education projects, people, events, and institutions.

Sue Ellen McCann, CAISE Senior Advisor and Executive Producer for KQED (standing far left), addresses a breakout group on media in STEM education.

Attendees nominate and vote for breakout topics during the Open Space sessions on Friday morning.

Nolan Doesken of the The Community Collaborative Rain, Hail and Snow Network project talks with John McLaughlin of NOAA during breakfast on Friday morning.

Elisabeth Soep, Senior producer of Youth Radio explains her Do It! project poster to Vera Michalchik of SRI International.

Elizabeth Farnsworth, PI for Go-Botany, shares her project poster with Chris Myers.

Jenny Lam, PI of the IMX (I AM X) discusses her poster during poster sessions.

Geoffrey Schladow (left) shares his 3D visualization tool of freshwater resources.

Kea Anderson of SRI International reviews the nominated topics for Open Space sessions on Friday afternoon.

CAISE Co-PI Kevin Crowley announces topics for Open Space sessions. Each nominator is invited to explain their interests to the meeting attendees.

Kate Taylor of WGBH Educational Foundation consults with NSF ISE Program Officer Valentine Kass.

Monya Ruffin, NSF Program Officer, offers her reflections on the PI Meeting and the frontiers of Informal Science Education.

Dennis Schatz, pictured with CAISE advisor Bruce Lewenstein and Valentine Kass, delivers his closing comments.

CAISE Project Director and Principal Investigator Jamie Bell, thanks meeting attendees and delivers his final closing comments.
